Model Information

Translation

Our objective was to create a model capable of enhancing transcribed Arabic texts to establish an accurate Palestinian Israeli dialect in Arabic.
We used a pretrained summarization model called AraBART.
Our training data consisted of 70,000 pairs of source and target texts. The source texts were transcripts produced by Azure Video Indexer AI, while the target texts were human-made transcriptions.
The model was trained on a single GPU for 3 epochs, taking approximately 4 hours to complete training.

Results

We evaluated the modelâ€™s performance by comparing the translation outputs to the true human-made translations. The results indicate a substantial improvement over the original transcriptions in terms of accuracy, as measured by BLEU and ROUGE scores.

Comparison of Translation and True Translation

  • BLEU Score: Mean: 0.7578, Median: 1.0
  • ROUGE-1: Mean: 0.9243, Median: 1.0
  • ROUGE-2: Mean: 0.8333, Median: 1.0

Comparison of Original and True Translation

  • BLEU Score: Mean: 0.6518, Median: 0.6690
  • ROUGE-1: Mean: 0.8828, Median: 0.9412
  • ROUGE-2: Mean: 0.7572, Median: 0.8235

These results demonstrate a significant improvement, particularly when examining the median scores, which reach near-perfect values for the model-generated translations.

Error Reduction

By defining the error as the difference between the score and a perfect match (1), we observe the following error reductions in the model-generated translations compared to the original:

  • BLEU: Error reduced by 30.5%
  • ROUGE-1: Error reduced by 35.5%
  • ROUGE-2: Error reduced by 31.5%
